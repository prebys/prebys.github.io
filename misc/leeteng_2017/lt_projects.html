2. Development of Stripling Kicker for APS

Mentor(s): Zachary Conway
Student: Yifan Su, Cornell University

The newly proposed Advanced Photon Source Upgrade storage ring will operate with a transverse electron beam size < 100 pm, much less than the present value of 3.1 mm.  This small beam size is not compatible with traditional techniques of injecting electrons into a storage ring which rely on multi-turn damping.  To overcome this limitation we are developing a set of new nano-second rise-time strip-line kickers to transfer the 6 GeV electron beam from the injector to stable orbits within the proposed storage ring.  In this project, the interested student will help with the design of a new strip-line kicker and with measurements characterizing geometric perturbations on our initial prototype.  It is expected that the student is comfortable with electrodynamics and interested in learning to use our existing software simulation packages.

Requirements:

  • Basic lab skills

    3.  Analysis of Cross-correlator

    Mentor(s): Jeff Dooling, Yine Sun
    Student: Diego Gutieriez Coronel, Illinois Institute of Technology

    Analyze the response of a combined auto- and cross-correlator (ACC) optical system used for measuring the green and UV laser pulse duration of the Advanced Photon Source photocathode (PC) drive laser system. Data from the ACC provides important information on UV laser’s longitudinal distribution. Together with the transverse profile, the 3D shape of the drive laser plays a critical role as the initial conditions of electron bunch emission from the PC rf gun. The space charge force of the electron beam drives the need for emittance compensation and preservation at the front end of the linac (prior to acceleration after the gun), and affects the achievable transverse emittances (εx, εy) from the gun. We seek to maximize the brightness (~I/(εx εy)) of the PC Gun beam. The student will not be operating or working with the high-power drive laser, but may use low-power lasers (Class 2).

    Requirements:

    • A good understanding of electromagnetics and optics

    4.  Charge Measurement with Beam Position Monitor

    Mentor(s): John Power
    Student: Chuan Yin, University of Chicago

    The Argonne Wakefield Accelerator (AWA) group is developing a compact, low-cost, complete Beam Position Monitor (BPM) system which consists of three components [(i) signal pickup device, (ii) processing electronics, and (iii) a simple readout] aimed at medical accelerators and small-scale accelerator facilities.  The BPM system will be capable of measuring both the 0th moment (charge) and the 1st moment (horizontal & vertical position).   The project for the student will be to develop the processing electronics with guidance from AWA accelerator scientists.
    The signal pickup device is an in-flange button-type BPM purchased from MDC vacuum which is already installed on the AWA witness beamline.  Given the short duration of the summer student’s stay, this will allow the student to make quick progress.

    Requirements:
    • Completed a course in electromagnetism
    • Basic understanding of accelerator physics
    • Programming skills (e.g. Matlab, Mathematica, etc.)

    5. Design of a Novel 300GHz Wave Guide

    Mentor(s): Kamlesh Suthar
    Student: Hector Perez, IIT

    Accelerator Science division is developing a novel corrugated waveguide that operates at 300GHz. The inner diameter of beam tube is 2mm and the internal corrugated structures are about 100 microns in size. The challenge of designing the structure lies in fabrication and assembly since the entire 1m-long structure may not be built in one piece and shorter pieces may be needed to be joined to produce a long structure. Initial RF simulation indicates that roughly 20W/cm power is expected to be distributed during operation. This structure is required to be cooled by water circulating tube attached next to this main structure. The impedance of wall is responsible for the heating.
       

    The student will work in a team of physicists and engineers to analyze this structure using finite element analysis software and optimize the thermo-mechanical design via integration of responsible physics.

    Requirements:

    • Multi-physics simulation
    • Finite element analysis
    • Novel fabrication techniques

Users’ Guide for Geneva Underground Playhouse Light Controller


This document is meant as a simple guide to familiarize users with the Chauvet StageDesigner 50 DMX light controller as it’s configured for the Geneva Underground Playhouse. It's not meant to replace the manual, and doesn't include initial setup instructions or other information not relevant to use in the theater.  It's meant for those who will be programming the board, as well as those who will be using the board after it has been programmed by someone else.  The full manual can be found here.

All users are encouraged to carefully read the Startup, Shutdown, and Troubleshooting sections before using the board.

Introduction

The light controller is set up to control up to 12 lights, via two DMX 6-channel dimmer packs.  Channels 1-6 are in the pack on Stage Right , and channels 7-12 are in the pack on Stage Left.  The actual mapping of the lights to the channels often changes from show to show, although channel 12 will always control the house lights.

In general, there are two modes of operation for the board:
  • Direct (“12 Channel A/B”) Mode – This is the simple, non-programmed mode, in which the sliders control the lights directly.  Sliders 1-12 (top) form bank A, while sliders 13-24 (bottom) form bank B and control the same lights.  Each bank is controlled by its own master fader.  Thus, while a scene is displayed from one bank, the next scene can be set up in the other bank.
  • Programmed (“Chase<->Scene”) Mode – In this mode, complete sets of light levels are stored as “steps”, then one or more steps are stored together as a "scene". Each scene is then assigned to one of the sliders in the bottom bank, with the overall level set by master fader B. Scenes with a single step are referred to as "static" scenes. Multi-step scenes can play out in a timed sequence, stepped thropugh manually, or synchronized to music. 

Startup

There's no power switch on the board.  It's usually left plugged into the power strip and comes on along with everything else when the power strip is turned on.  It always comes up in “blackout” mode, indicated by the green LED above the “blackout” button.  Push the "blackout" button to make the LED go out.

When the board is first powered up and brought out of blackout, the lights will still be out, regardless of the position of the sliders. A level or scene will not be activated until the associated slider is moved.  For this reason, it's generally best to start with all the sliders at "0".

Shutdown

Powering down the light board does not shut the lights off!In fact, it might send the lights to full on and trip the breakers. Set all sliders to "0", hit the BLACKOUT button, and then power down the board, either by uplugging the power strip or turning it off.

IMPORTANT:  Even if the lights are blacked out, there's a glitch that occasionally causes one or more of them to turn on when the board is powered down.  If this happens, power the board up, toggle the  BLACKOUT button off and on, and shut it down again.

Our lights are very old and get very hot.  Never, under any circumstances, leave stage lights on when the theater is unattended.  If all else fails, the lights can be shut off at the breaker panel in the HVAC closet at the opposite corner from the tech booth.  The two breakers which control the dimmer packs are marked with blue tape.
 

Non-Programmed (1-12 A/B) Operation

The board can be operated in direct, non-programmed mode even if it's been programmed for a different application.  To set up for non-programmed operation:
  1. Verify that the BLACKOUT LED is not lit.  If it is, tap the "BLACKOUT" button until it's extinquished.
  2. Use the "MODE" button to set the MODE to "(1-12 A/B)" (yellow LED) mode.
Generally, you will want to set the "FADE" speed to INSTANT (UP), or some very short value, and control the speed with the individual sliders or master faders.  Setting the FADE speed too slow is a common way of convincing yourself the board isn't working.

In this mode, the A bank (1-12, top) and the B bank (13-24, bottom) each control channels 1 through 12.  The overall ampltude is set by the A and B master faders just to the right of the banks.  In general, while a scene is displayed from one bank, the fader for the other bank is set to zero, so the next scene can be set up.  The A fader has the maximum up and the B has the maximum down to simplify cross-fading from one scent to the next, although both are independent, so any combination of the A and B banks can be displayed.

Programming

Each set of light levels can be saved as a separate "scene" or as individual "steps" within a scene.  In "Mix<->Chase" playback mode, each scene is assigned to one of the 12 sliders in the "B" (bottom) bank, with the "B" master fader controller the overall intensity.  The steps within a scene can play out in a timed sequence, can be synchronized with music, or can be stepped through manually.  Scenes that contain only a single step are referred to as "static" scenes.

Since there are 12 sliders in the bank, up to 12 scenes can be controlled at a time.  These can be selected from 4 pages, for a total of 48 scenes in memory.  Each scene can have up to 1000 steps, for a total of 48000 individual lighting states.

Lighting transitions in a show can be done either by switching from one static scene to another or by stepping through multiple steps withing a scene or scenes. The choice is up to the light designer programming the board, but in most cases it's easier to do it as a series of static scenes.  Cases where multi-step scenes are appropriate include:
  • Shows or parts of a show with a very large number of different lighting configurations, such that having 12 scenes available at a time is not enough.
  • Instances with rapid and/or automated lighting sequences.
It's worth remembering that the "A" (top) bank continues to control individual lights.  Among other things, this can be used to "sneak" individual lights in and out of a scene. In some cases, this could be an alternative to complex programming sequences.

The general procedure for programming is to record a step or sequencial series of steps to temporary memory and then assign them to a particular page and scene number.  The procedure is the same for all of the different playback modes.

Programming Steps and Scenes

  1. Use the "MODE" button to set the mode to "1-24A" (green LED).
  2. Set the "FADE" slider to "INSTANT" (UP).
  3. To put the board into RECORD mode, while holding the "RECORD"button, tap "flash" buttons (buttons under the sliders) 1, 5,6, and 8 in order, then release the "RECORD" button.  All the blue leds above the sliders should FLASH once, indicating the board is in record mode.
  4. To program a step into local memory, set the sliders to their desired configuration and then tap the "RECORD" button. All the blue LEDs should FLASH and the display should indicate "STEP 1".
  5. If there are multiple steps in the scene, repeat (3) until they have all been loaded.  The step count on the display should increment each time.
  6. Use the "PAGE" button to select the page (1 to 4) where you want to store the scene.
  7. While holding the "RECORD" button, tap the "FLASH" button below the slider for which you want to load the scene. All blue LEDs should flash, indicating the scene has been loaded.
  8. Loading the scene clears the local memory and resets the step counter to zero in preparation for programming the next scene.  If you make a mistake, you can manually clear the local memory by holding the "RECORD" button and tapping "PAGE/REC CLR".
  9. Repeat (4 through 8) for any additional scenes.
  10. Exit RECORD mode by holding the "RECORD" button and tapping "REC EXIT".
Note that temporary memory is erased when you exit record mode or power down the board.  Scenes remain stored until they are erased or replaced.

Editing Steps

  1. Use "MODE" button to set the mode to "Chase<->Scene" (red LED). Yes, this is different than when recording scenes!
  2. Set master fader B to max, so you can see the effect of your edits!
  3. To put the board into RECORD mode, while holding the "RECORD"button, tap "flash" buttons (buttons under the sliders) 1, 5,6, and 8 in order, then release the "RECORD" button.  All the blue leds above the sliders should FLASH once, indicating the board is in record mode.
  4. While holding the "EDIT" buttons, tap the FLASH button for the scene you wish to edit.  The yellow LED above the scene should come on and the display should read "EDITING".  
  5. Set the scene's slider to max so you can see the effect of the edits on the scene.
  6. If necessary, press "STEP" to select the step within the scene which you wish to edit.
  7. To raise (lower) a level hold the UP (DOWN) button, then press and hold the FLASH button of the channel you wish to change.  The display will show the changing level (0-254) of the light setting.  Hold both buttons until the desired level is reached, then release.
  8. Repeat (7) for any other levels in the step.
  9. Repeat (4) through (8) for any other scenes and steps you wish to edit.
  10. Exit EDIT mode by holding the "RECORD" button and tapping "REC EXIT".
  11. Exit RECORD mode by holding the "RECORD" button and tapping "REC EXIT" a second time.

Pre-programmed Operation

To set up the board for programmed playback:
  1. Press the "MODE" button to put the board in "Chase<->Scene" (red LED) mode.
  2. Press the "PARK" button to put the board in "Mix Chase" (yellow LED) mode.  This will allow different scenes to be mixed and to allow one to cross-fade one scene to another.
Now each of the bank B (lower) 12 sliders controls a scene, programmed as in the previous section.  There are four pages of scenes, selected with the "PAGE" button and indicated by the LEDs below.  A scene starts when its slider is moved to a non-zero value or when its FLASH button is tapped or held.  You can cross-fade from one scene to another by simultaneously lowering one scene's slider and raising the other.  If you switch pages while a scene is playing, that scene will continue to play, rather than switching immediately to the scene stored on the new page.  In this way, you can cross-face scenes between pages unless they have the same scene number.  To switch to a scene with the same number on a different page, you would have to switch pages and lower the slider to 0 (in either order).  Raising the slider again will load the new scene.

If a scene has multiple steps, there are several ways to step through them.  In all cases, the steps will continue to cycle through the programmed sequence indefinitely; that is, they will not step when they get to the last step.

Also for all cases, the FADE speed slider will control how fast one step transitions to the next.  Most of the range of the slider is beyond the range that would normally be used.  Setting the FADE too slow might give you the impression the board is not working.
  • Manually
    1. If the green AUDIO LED is lit, tap the "AUDIO" button to extinguish it
    2. Slide the SPEED lever to "SHOW MODE" (down).  The LED above the STEP button should not be flashing.  If it is, slide the SPEED lever up and back down to extinguish it.
    3. Use the STEP button to step through the sequence.  The current step number will show on the display. NOTE: if you step past the desired step, there is no way to go back.  You will need to either cycle through or start the scene over!!

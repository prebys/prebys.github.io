Notes on Life at CERN

A lot of other people are more knowledgeable, but here are some of my random notes about what do while one is working at CERN.

General

France is a bit cheaper than Geneva, when it comes to living or eating out.  There used to be a bigger difference, but it's still true at some level. 

Geneva has a great public transportation system, and driving in the city should be avoided at all costs.

Restaurants

Lots and lots.  Here are a few of my favorites.

Restaurant 1 at CERN

Not a great restaurant, but they have beer and wine.  It's a good place to meet and figure out where to go next

Le Bois Joly

This is an absolute must at least once!  Up the hills toward the Jura.  Good food, deent prices, and an absolutely incredible view.  You can make a day of it and take the telepherique and hike the Jura, then finish up with dinner.  Reservations for the deck recommended on nice days.

Le Coq Rouge


Right in St. Genis, just across the French border.  On the same square with Charly's Pub (which you'll get to know).  Good food, and the prices aren't bad.  The lower priced fixed menu is usually plenty, and they have a great cheese cart for dessert.

Cafe Restaurant de L'Aviation

A CERN tradition.  As the name suggests, it's near the airport.  Their specialty is a steak frites with a heavy garlic cream pepper sauce, called a "caravelle".  It take some time off  your life, but it's worth it.  The trick is that is long as there's steak on your plate, they'll keep bringing you more frites and sauce (although they're much stingier than they used to be.).

L'Azzura Pizzeria

Nice, reasonably priced pizza place in Ferney Voltaire, France. 

Rajpoute Indian Restaurant

Near Ferney Voltaire, in France.   There are a lot more Indian restaurants than there used to be, but when CERN people say "The Indian Restaurant", this is the one they mean (or may not anymore; I guess there's one right by the lab). NB: I lived in the Vert Village housing development accross the street from 90-92.

Bain de Paquis

This is a hidden gem.  A lot of CERN people don't even know about it.  It's not really a restaurant.  It's a swimming area on a little artificial island on Lake Geneva, but the cafe has good food at a good price, and the it's a great place to hang out on a nice day.  You have to pay a franc or two to get in, but it's worth it, whether you swim or not.  Not to be confused with "The Paquis", which is the nearby red light district.

Places to visit in and around Geneva

Lots of nice places to visit in Geneva, and these days the tram goes all the way from CERN.  Definitely don't drive, because parking can be a nightmare.

Cornavin (train station)/Pont du Mont Blanc/Quai Wilson

Take the tram to the train station and walk down to the lake.  Lots of bars and restaurants.  Touristy but fun.

Old Town

Old walled part of Geneva.  On a hill accross the Pont du Mont Blanc from the train station (easy walk).  Great to walk around and sight see.  Some very good (and very expensive) restaurants.  There's an old church, which isn't much by European standards (the Calvanists were pretty spartan decorators), but there are some Roman ruins underneath it that are very cool to visit.

Plainpalais

Area near the University of Geneva.  You can get there by tram.  Nice place to hang out.  Lots of restaurants and pubs that are reasonably priced, but Geneva standards.

Ferney-Voltaire

The "big" Frence town across the border.  Nice place to walk around.  You can get there by bus from CERN.  Lots of restaurants and cafes.  They have an enormous farmers' market on Saturdays that you should go to at least once (but beware of pickpockets). 

Fun places to visit outside of Geneva

Chamonix

This is a must while you're at CERN.  It's a tourist town near the Mont Blanc Tunnel.  You can drive there from Geneva, or get there by train.  Cool town with beautiful view and lots of nice restaurants.  You should take the cog wheel train to the Mer de Glace glacier.  They carve tunnels into the glacier, which are cool in a tacky sort of way.  If it's a nice day, you can take the cog wheel train up and hike back down, or hike the whole thing if you're really up for it.  You can also take the telepherique to the Aguille du Midi, if you have the time (and money!). 

La Gruyere

Yes, where the cheese comes from.  Obviously, you'll want to eat a fondue while you're there.  It's an almost absurdly picturesque town, except...
...right in the middle of it is the H.R. Giger museum.  He's the talented and deeply disturbed artist who designed the sets and creatures for "Alien".  The cafe looks like the spaceship from the movie.  Definitely a must while you're there.  I don't know how to get there without a car.

Chateau de Chillon

Very cool old castle on Lake Geneva.  The pictures are all carefully photographed to make it look like it's in the middle of nowhere, so you'll be a little disappointed when you arrive and realize it's in a pretty developed area, but once you get inside, it's really fascinating.

Annecy

Cool French tourist town.  A fun place to spend a day.

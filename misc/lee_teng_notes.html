General

The Lee Teng internship is a 10 week program that includes two weeks at the USPAS.  It's a joint program with Argonne, in which we usually accept 5 interns at each lab.  The selection is handled by a joint committee, after which the programs are adminstered separately at the two labs.  We line up the nominal dates of the program with the DOE/SULI program, because it makes logistics easier at Fermilab, where students are grouped in off site housing.  This means they either start one or two weeks before the USPAS, depending on the year.  That schedule doesn't accomodate tri-mester schools, so we have historically allowed such students to shift their schedule a week or two, provided they arrive in to to take the full two-week USPAS course.

I have four mailing lists related to the program:
  • leetenginternship@fnal.gov:  This is the official contact address.  It goes to the program director and Rosa
  • leetengselection@fnal.gov:  This is used for communication among the selection committee
  • leeteng_fermilab@fnal.gov:  Interns and mentors for fermilab, as well as program director and Rosa
  • leeteng_argonne@fnal.gov: Interns and mentors at Argonne, plus Linda Spentzsouris
  • leeteng_alumni@fnal.gov: Every year, I move the students from the lab lists to the alumni list, prior to adding the new students.  I ocassionally reach out to this list, to see what people, but in many cases the email address they had at the time they were interns no longer works.
I have added you to all the lists and made you an owner.  I have left myself on for the moment, but you can take me off when I'm no longer useful.

At Fermilab, all the practical details of the internship (travel, housing, cars, etc) are handled by Rosa Foote, who is also handling all the other internships, except SIST.  This makes your life a lot easier!  Things are a little more complicated at Argonne.  Linda Spentzouris is your primary contact, and the details are handled by a number of different people.

Every year, we've made posters to advertise the program, and I sent them out to a mailing list that has grown over the years.  Last year, I added "How did you hear about the program?" to the application, and the vast majority were divided between "word of mouth" and "internet search", with only two selecting "poster" - so spending money to print and mail posters is probably not the best use of resources. 

You can decide if you want to produce them and send email copies to people.  If so, I normally work with Diana Brandonisio in VMS, although the request has to go through Karen Seifrid. Diana has a template, and every year she just updates the USPAS part, uses the student picture from the previous year, and changes the background color.

Application

I'm overjoyed to dump this issue in someone else's lap!

We have traditionally used an SQL script written by Liz Quigg. It's not perfect, but it did the job.  A couple years ago, HR started pushing the internships to start using their HODES system.  At the time, HODES was completely inadequate for our needs (e.g. no way to submit reference letters).  They tried to simply force it on everyone, but the internships banded together to stop them.  Tim ironed out an agreement where they would work with the internships to come up with an acceptable program - which of course never happened.  Last year, they tried to force their system - which by then had been improved - on everyone.  Sasha Valishev and I refused to use it because we didn't have enough time to properly evaluate it, and continued to use Liz's system.  Elliott McCrory did use the new system for SIST, and I guess it wasn't all that horrible, but you might also want to talk to Sandra Charles.

Anyway, Liz either has or will retire, so using her system is no longer an option.  The right answer would be to use Academic Jobs Online, but that meets with a lot of resistance (for reasons I don't understand).  Whatever you decide, you and Sasha Valishev should form a united front on this (IMHO). 

Application Materials

We ask for:
  • Basic information
  • an essay
  • Two letters
  • An unofficial transcript. We used to ask for an official one, but that costs money and takes time, so we stopped.  We reserve the right to ask for an official one if they are accepted.

Deadline

We originally set our deadline to be the same as SULI, which was around Feb 8th.  Over the last few years, I noticed that our yield (particularly for women) was going down, because they had already accepted other internships.  I checked and the SULI deadline had been creeping up, so last year I reset ours to match at January 13th.  As a result, the number of applications went down dramatically, but our yield did go back up.  I think that's a little early, so I'd split the difference and make it something like January 20th, but of course that's your call.

Mentors and Selection

This is entirely the new director's call, but here's what we did...

We bring the mentors into the selection process.  This is a real pain, but both the mentors and the students seem to be happier with the matches.  This means picking the mentors before or early in the selection process.  We tend to draw from the same pool of mentors, although there's pressure from the Directorate to branch out a bit.  I normally lined up the Fermilab mentors myself, and you can find a list of previous mentors by going to the pages above and clicking on the "Projects" links.  Linda Spentzouris lines up the mentors at Argonne.

Last year's selection committee was:
  • Fermilab
    • me (Chair)
    • Giulio Stancari
    • Mayling Wong-Squires
  • Argonne
    • Curt Preissner
    • Tim Berenc
    • Linda Spentzouris (really IIT, but paid by Argonne for this role)
They can be contacted via the leetengselection@fnal.gov mailing list.

Our selection process varied a bit, but it normally went something like this
  • I assigned every application two readers.  I randomized it so the same two readers didn't read the same applications.  I tweaked things so engineering applicants would be read by an engineer with a similar background.
  • Readers divided applicants into top, mid, and bottom.
  • We got together and used this for form a first cut.  Normally cases where both readers put someone in the top were kept, as second group would be read by the entire group.  Our goal was to end up with a short list of ~20 students.
  • From this group, we found 4 or 5 best matches for each of the projects and send these to the mentor, who would rank order them.
    • NOTE!  Make sure the mentors know they only have to read those applications!  No matter how clear I was on this, a couple of mentors would read ALL the applications at this point.
  • I would try to match students to mentors.  Usually, every mentor would get their first or second choice,.
At this point, I would send out email acceptances, with a 7-10 day deadline.  From this point on, the formal hiring process was separate at each lab.
  • At Fermilab, Rosa Foote manages ALL logistics, including offers, travel, housing, and cars.  
  • Things are a bit more complicated at Argonne.  Linda Spentzouris shepherds the process, and Lisa Reed handles a lot of stuff, but parts are handled within the divisions of the individual mentors.  You should just check into things once in a while, to make sure nothing fell through the cracks.  

Visa Issues

Suffice to say you should keep on top of this issue yourself, rather than assuming someone else will deal with it. I can't stress this enough.

Lee Teng accepts students legally attending US universities, regardless of nationality, with the exception of State Sponsors of Terror (Libya, Soudan, and Syria).  Foreign students are responsible for satisfying any visa formalities required to work at a summer internship.  Last year, I put together this guide for foreign students.  You should become familiar with it and keep it up to date, as some things (e.g. DACA) may change.

If you have questions, Valery Stanley in the Visa Office is a very good resource. 

USPAS

Attending the USPAS is an important part of the program.  In the past, we've gotten some money to at least partically support the Lee Teng students, but the exact amount has varied, and the general trend has been downward.  You should talk to Susan Winchester at USPAS to see what the arrangement will be this year.  Make sure she gets the list of students as soon as you have it.

Calendar and Deliverables

The calendar has undergone some tweaking.  Basically, we like events that involve all the interns, but don't want to cut into their work time any more than we already do.  There's also an issue of maintaining an appropriate balance between the two labs.  If you take a look at this calendar, you'll see what we arrived at.  The main features are:
  • Fermilab visit near the beginning, including tours.  I try to coordinate this with the Users' Meeting if possible, because this can often result in free food.
  • U of Chicago visit some time in the middle.  This year, we did it on a Saturday to minimize the impact, and no one complained.
    • Make arrangements with Emily Whitman Easton at U of C.
    • At Fermilab, we have traditionally coordinated the trip with the Helen Edwards (formerly PARTI) students.  Talk to Sasha Valishev about this.
  • Argonne students come to Fermilab to take part in the Fermilab intern poster sessions (this was considered a major success by everyone).  No tours this time.
  • Fermilab students go to Argonne to do tours and deliver their final talks. 
So the deliverables are a talk, a poster, and a paper.  I always set up a single Indico page (located on the calendar),  where students can upload all of these.

There are also a series of events for all interns, organized by Rosa.  You can advise the Lee Teng students which ones  you think are important, given that they have more distractions than the other interns.

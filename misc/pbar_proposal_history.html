A Brief History or pBar Proposals


The current PAC agenda can be found here.  The latest encarnation of the antiproton proposal is P-1022. This new proposal is an updated version of P-986, which was presented to the PAC three times, with progressively more negative results:
  • March, 2009
    • Letter of Intent
    • Recommendation: "To proceed further, it would be necessary to substantially develop the physics case and
      the plan for the detector configuration, and strengthen the Collaboration. " (report, p. 3)
  • June, 2009 (agenda no longer online)
    • Updated information (couldn't find actual presentation)
    • Recommendation: "The case presented is not strong enough to warrant the investment required." (report, p. 8)
  • Nov, 2010
    • Presented as Now called TApAS
    • Recommendation: "The Committee does not recommend that this proposal go forward under either of the
      funding scenarios in the charge" (See report, p. 5)
Prior to this, Dan proposed P-981, to study the gravitational acceleration of anti-hydrogen. It was presented to the PAC twice, and rejected:
  • Mar, 2008
    • Proposal
    • Recommendation: "The Committee recommends that the proponents present quantitative evidence that they
      have the method fully developed and implemented for normal atoms" (report, p.4)
  • Jun, 2008 (agenda no longer online)
    • Updated (presentation no longer online)
    • "The Committee does not recommend that the Laboratory further consider this experiment." (report, p.6)  
These were the first proposals to the PAC that I could find, but a letter of intent was submitted to the Fermilab Steering Committee in June 2007, for a "Low- and Medium Energy Antiproton Physics at Fermilab". It is not mentioned in the final proposed plan.

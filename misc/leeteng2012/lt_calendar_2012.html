2012 Lee Teng Summer Internship Program

General Preparation

It's a good idea for students and mentors to contact each other regarding the details of the project and any suggested preparation.  The list of projects, mentors, and students can be found here.

The USPAS course packs a semester's worth of material into two weeks, so it moves very quickly and it's easy to get overwhelmed.  It helps a lot to go in with at least a conceptual understanding of the material.  Some good resources are:
  • The lectures notes from last year.  This will give you some idea what you're up against.
  • The Fermilab "Concepts Rookie Book".  This is part of a series of  books written to train accelerator operators at Fermilab.  Much of the material is specific to Fermilab, but sections II-V of this book cover general topics in accelerator physics.
As you can see from the schedule below, each student will be expected to give two short presentations and to submit a written report about their work over the summer.  Last year's final presentations can be found here, and examples of written reports from all previous years can be found here.

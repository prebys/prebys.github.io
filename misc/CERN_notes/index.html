Eric's Notes on Life at CERN

Here are some of my random notes about what do while one is working at CERN, with the caveat that some of my information may be out of date, and other people know a lot more.

General

You can find a general guide for newcomers here.

Remember, you'll cross the border a lot between Switzerland and France.  Switzerland has never joined the EU, and still uses Swiss Francs instead of Euros.  A lot of places will take both, but generally give you a bad rate on cash.  Credit/cash cards are better, or you get local money from cash machines.  If you don't have a PIN for your credit card, call and see if they'll send you one, because European stores expect it.  You can usually manage without it, but it will complicate transactions a bit, particularly if you don't speak French very well.  Speaking of which...

You can get by pretty well in English at CERN and in Geneva, but if you go elsewhere, you'll definitely want to learn some basic French. 

Although they didn't join the EU, Switzerland did join the "Schengen Agreement" on common immigration a few years ago, which made things much easier. It means there's no immigration control at the border any more, and you no longer have to go through Swiss imimgration if you transfer at another European airport. 

France is a bit cheaper than Geneva, when it comes to living or eating out.  There used to be a bigger difference, but it's still true at some level. 

Geneva has a great public transportation system, and the tram now comes all the way to CERN. Driving in the city should be avoided at all costs.  There's a bus which will take you to St. Genis and Ferney-Voltaire in France, but it doesn't run very often.

Restaurants

Lots and lots.  Here are a few of my favorites.

Restaurant 1 at CERN

Not greatest restaurant, but not bad, and they have beer and wine, It's also kind of the center of social life at the lab.  People tend to gather there after work and have a few drinks, and it will often evolve into a plan for the evening.  If you're a....more mature...person, you invariably run into people you haven't seen in years.

Le Bois Joly

This is an absolute must at least once!  Up the hills toward the Jura.  Good food, decent prices, and an absolutely incredible view.  You can make a day of it and take the telepherique to hike the Jura, then finish up with dinner.  Reservations for the deck recommended on nice days.

Le Coq Rouge

Right in St. Genis, just across the French border.  On the same square with Charly's Pub (which you'll get to know).  Good food, and the prices aren't bad.  The lower priced fixed menu is usually plenty, and they have a great cheese cart for dessert.

Cafe Restaurant de L'Aviation

A CERN tradition.  As the name suggests, it's near the airport.  Their specialty is a steak frites with a heavy garlic cream pepper sauce, called a "caravelle".  It takes some years off  your life, but it's worth it.  The trick is that is long as there's steak on your plate, they'll keep bringing you more frites and sauce (although they're much stingier than they used to be.).

L'Azzura Pizzeria

Nice, reasonably priced pizza place in Ferney Voltaire, France. 

Bain de Paquis

This is a hidden gem.  A lot of CERN people don't even know about it.  It's not really a restaurant.  It's a swimming area on a little artificial island on Lake Geneva, but the cafe has good food at a good price, and the it's a great place to hang out on a nice day.  You have to pay a franc or two to get in, but it's worth it, whether you swim or not.  I learned about this place from a former colleague who left physics to go into lucrative career in Swiss banking - but is still so tight he squeaks.

Not to be confused with "The Paquis", which is the nearby red light district.  Note, there are also some nice bars and restaurants in the red light district, which - being Swiss - is perfectly safe.

Places to visit in and around Geneva

Lots of nice places to visit in Geneva, and these days the tram goes all the way from CERN.  Definitely don't drive, because parking can be a nightmare.

Cornavin (train station)/Pont du Mont Blanc/Quai Wilson

Take the tram to the train station, which opens onto a public mall that will lead you down to the lake.  Lots of bars and restaurants.  Touristy but fun.  Great place to people watch. Because of the large number of international organizations and Swiss neutrality, you'll see groups and combinations of people that are literally not possible anywhere else in the world.

Of course, the big landmark in that area is the Jet d'Eau in the lake.

Old Town

Old walled part of Geneva.  On a hill accross the Pont du Mont Blanc from the train station (easy walk).  Great to walk around and sight see.  Some very good (and very expensive) restaurants.  There's an old church, which isn't much by European standards (the Calvanists were pretty spartan decorators), but there are some Roman ruins underneath it that are very cool to visit.

Plainpalais

Area near the University of Geneva.  You can get there by tram.  Nice place to hang out.  Lots of restaurants and pubs that are reasonably priced, by Geneva standards.  There's a market there on weekends, and frequently other activities.

Ferney-Voltaire

The "big" French town across the border.  Nice place to walk around.  You can get there by bus from CERN.  Lots of restaurants and cafes.  They have an enormous farmers' market on Saturdays that you should go to at least once (but beware of pickpockets).  You can also visit Voltaire's house.

Fun places to visit outside of Geneva

Chamonix

This is a must while you're at CERN.  It's a tourist town near the Mont Blanc Tunnel.  You can drive there from Geneva, or get there by train.  Cool town with beautiful view and lots of nice restaurants.  You should take the cog wheel train to the Mer de Glace glacier.  They carve tunnels into the glacier, which are cool in a tacky sort of way.  If it's a nice day, you can take the cog wheel train up and hike back down, or hike the whole thing if you're really hardcore.  You can also take the telepherique to the Aguille du Midi, if you have the time (and money!). 

La Gruyere

Yes, where the cheese comes from.  Obviously, you'll want to eat a fondue while you're there.  It's an almost absurdly picturesque town, except...
...right in the middle of it is the H.R. Giger museum.  He's the talented and deeply disturbed artist who designed the sets and creatures for "Alien".  The cafe looks like the spaceship from the movie.  Definitely a must while you're there.  I don't know how to get there without a car.

Chateau de Chillon

Very cool old castle on Lake Geneva.  The pictures are all carefully photographed to make it look like it's in the middle of nowhere, so you'll be a little disappointed when you arrive and realize it's in a pretty developed area, but once you get inside, it's really fascinating.

Annecy

Cool French tourist town.  A fun place to spend a day when the weather's nice.  I think you can get there by train.

Clubs

CERN has a huge number of clubs that are subsiized by the lab.  I you have an interest, the chances are good that there's a club for it, and that it's a great deal.  For example, when I worked there I joined the Automobile Club, which included hydraulic lifts and a full time mechanic, and the Music Club, which had a practice area with amps, micophones, a PA system, and even digital recording equipment (in 1990!).

The Ski Club is a particularly good deal, if you're there through the winter.

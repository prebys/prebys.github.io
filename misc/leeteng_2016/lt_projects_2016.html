Argonne Projects

1.  Characterization of an In-situ metrology setup for the APS Modular Deposition System (MDS)

Mentor(s): Lahsen Assoufid
Student: Jayson Shiau, Northern Illinois University

The candidate will participate, within a team of experts, in testing and evaluating the performance of a high speed interferometer to be used for the in-situ metrology subsystem of the Advanced Photon Source' (APS) newly-acquired Modular Deposition System (MDS). The MDS is a sophisticated thin film sputtering tool designed for R&D and fabrication of next-generation thin film optics for the APS beamlines. In-situ metrology will allow the system's operator to monitor the surface quality of an optic after each predefined number of successive coating and figure-correction steps, without removing it out of the vacuum chamber and disturbing the mirror in-situ mounting and setup.

Requirements:
  • Mechanical engineering and design or physic
  • Basics optics
  • Computer programing in Python, Matlab or IDL.

2. Digital RF Technology on a MicroTCA Platform

Mentor(s): Tim Berenc
Student: Eugene Wu, Northwestern University

Radio-frequency (rf) systems employ feedback control to regulate both the amplitude and phase of the accelerating voltage in a particle accelerator. The Advanced Photon Source is exploring FPGA-based digital rf technology using a MicroTCA platform (Micro-Telecommunications Computing Architecture) for various rf feedback applications.

This hands-on project will entail implementing an adaptive notch filter [1-2] with a MicroTCA system consisting of baseband analog-to-digital (A/D) and digital-to-analog (D/A) converter FPGA Mezzanine Cards (FMCs) and a FPGA-based MicroTCA FMC carrier board. Additionally, a high-speed A/D and D/A rf transceiver board will be explored for intermediate frequency (IF) or direct rf sampling applications.

This is a great opportunity to be exposed to rf systems for particle accelerators and get practical hands-on experience with modern high-speed digital computing platform technology being used in a wide range of industry applications.

Requirements:

  • Electrical engineering & computer sciences student with coursework in feedback control theory, digital signal processing, and experience with FPGA programming.
  • Strong motivation and independence in experiment and data analysis.

3.  Tuning Software for ATLAS

Mentor(s): Brahim Mustapha and Clay Dickerson
Student: Raheem Barnett, Princeton University

We have recently developed an online model for the tuning and operation of the ATLAS linac. ATLAS is a DOE National User Facility at Argonne’s Physics Division. The online model is based on the beam dynamics code TRACK, which has also been developed at the Physics Division. While the model is ready it is yet to be tested both offline and online. Offline tests are to check the software before testing it online for actual beam tuning in the linac. The interested student will participate in these offline and online tests as well as possible improvements in the software.

Requirements:

  • Strong physics background with analytical and computing skills.
  • Preferences: Basic beam physics knowledge and familiarity with Linux/Unix operating systems.

5. Simulations and Measurements of RF Accelerating Cavities

Mentor(s): Geoff Waldschmidt
Student: Alexander Pizzuto, Loyola University

The project will consist of electromagnetic simulations of accelerating cavities as well as rf measurements. Simulations will be used to characterize the performance parameters of the device and to evaluate the experimental setup.   The rf measurements will be performed to verify simulation results and assist in the measurement of higher order modes of the device.  Ultimately, the measurements will be used to evaluate an accelerating structure to determine the consequence of cavity dislocations due to gravitational effects followed by an evaluation of the structure after modifications have been made.  An automated, software-controlled data acquisition system will be used to acquire data and to post-process the results. 

Skills:
Labview programming and an understanding of electromagnetics.

2.  Performance Characterization of LCLS-II Superconducting Radiofrequency
cryomodules

Mentor(s): Elvin Harms
Student: Pond Sitorattanakul, Lehigh University

LCLS-II is a next generation x-ray free electron laser to be constructed

Fermilab is responsible for the design of the accelerator cryomodules

facility to cool down each cryomodule built at Fermilab to 2 Kelvin and

in spring/summer 2016 with the arrival of the first prototype
cryomodule. Graphical interfaces to run and monitor the tests as well as
software tools to aid in analysis of the test data and summarize the
results need to be developed.

  • Some software experience

4.  Diffusion measurement with transverse beam echoes

Mentor(s): Tanaji Sen
Student: Yuan Shen Li, Carleton College

Generating beam echoes is a novel method of quickly measuring beam diffusion. This can be done with a pulsed quadrupole and a measurement time of about a thousand turns. This contrasts with conventional collimator scans which can take hours.  Once proven to be effective, this would be applicable to all future hadron storage ring accelerators. The project will focus on simulating the feasibility of generating these echoes in Integrable Optics Test Accelerator (IOTA), which is being built at Fermilab. It will include developing theory and simulation tools for the experiment. Several machine configurations will be examined including the different nonlinear lenses and the implications of coupling. Different quadrupole pulse sequences such as multiple short pulses with alternating polarities will be studied for generating larger amplitude echoes and greater sensitivity to diffusion. A basic familiarity with beam optics (learned at the USPAS) and use of an optics program will be needed.

Requirements:

  • Analytical skills and expertise in a programming language will be helpful.
  • A basic familiarity with beam optics (learned at the USPAS) and use of an optics program will be needed.
  • Analytical skills and expertise in a programming language will be helpful.
